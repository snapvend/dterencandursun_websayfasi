What is Teeth Whitening?

Professional teeth whitening is designed to improve tooth brightness without altering natural form. Before planning the procedure, gum health, enamel structure, previous restorations, and sensitivity history are evaluated. At Archident, whitening protocols are selected according to the person's expectations and oral condition.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients bothered by tooth discoloration
  • People preparing for smile enhancement treatments
  • Cases with healthy gums and suitable enamel condition
  • Patients seeking a brighter but natural look

How is the treatment process planned?

The treatment starts with a clinical examination and cleaning when needed. A professional whitening protocol is then planned in-office, and recommendations are provided to protect comfort and help maintain the result.

What should be considered afterward?

Temporary sensitivity can occur after whitening. Following dietary advice, avoiding strong pigments for a short time, and maintaining regular oral care help support the final shade.

Teeth Whitening FAQ

Frequently asked questions about teeth whitening

Does whitening damage the teeth?

When whitening is planned professionally and under clinical supervision, it is performed with products selected for the condition of the teeth and gums.

Will there be sensitivity afterward?

Temporary sensitivity can occur after professional whitening, especially in patients already prone to sensitivity.

How long do whitening results last?

Longevity depends on oral hygiene, dietary habits, smoking, and routine maintenance. Periodic reassessment may be recommended.
