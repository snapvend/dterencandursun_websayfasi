What is Prosthetic Treatments?

Prosthetic treatment aims to restore missing teeth and support function, speech, and aesthetics. Depending on the case, fixed or removable options may be considered, and the plan is shaped by the number of missing teeth, remaining support, and patient expectations. At Archident, prosthetic planning is approached with a balance of comfort, durability, and appearance.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients missing one or more teeth
  • People seeking support for chewing and speech function
  • Cases requiring fixed or removable replacement planning
  • Patients who need individualized restorative sequencing

How is the treatment process planned?

The process begins with a clinical and radiographic evaluation of the remaining teeth, gums, and supporting structures. Treatment options are then reviewed and sequenced according to function, hygiene access, and aesthetic goals.

What should be considered afterward?

Whether fixed or removable, prosthetic restorations need daily care and regular professional follow-up to maintain fit, comfort, and hygiene.

Prosthetic Treatments FAQ

Frequently asked questions about prosthetic treatment

What types of prosthetic options are available?

Depending on the case, fixed restorations, removable dentures, or implant-supported prosthetic options may be considered.

How is the right prosthetic plan chosen?

The number of missing teeth, gum and bone support, hygiene needs, function, and aesthetic expectations are reviewed together before a decision is made.

Do prosthetic restorations require maintenance?

Yes. Regular professional checks and consistent home care are important to preserve fit, hygiene, and long-term comfort.
