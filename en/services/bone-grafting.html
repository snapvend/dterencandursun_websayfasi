What is Bone Grafting?

Bone grafting is planned to support areas where bone volume is insufficient for predictable implant treatment or stable restoration support. Different grafting approaches may be considered depending on the size, location, and clinical objective of the defect. At Archident, grafting decisions are made with a focus on long-term function and healing.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with limited bone support for implants
  • Cases requiring augmentation before prosthetic planning
  • Sites affected by long-term tooth loss or bone resorption
  • Patients assessed through detailed radiographic analysis

How is the treatment process planned?

The amount and location of the deficiency are reviewed first. A suitable grafting approach is then planned, and timing relative to implant placement is determined according to the patient's anatomy and treatment sequence.

What should be considered afterward?

Healing needs to be monitored carefully after bone grafting. Instructions about hygiene, diet, and follow-up visits help protect the grafted area during recovery.

Bone Grafting FAQ

Frequently asked questions about bone grafting

When is bone grafting needed?

It is considered when bone volume is not sufficient to support implant treatment or other restorative goals predictably.

Does grafting delay treatment?

In some cases healing time is needed before the next phase. The exact timeline depends on the size of the graft and the treatment plan.

Is bone grafting always combined with implants?

Not always. It may be planned before implants or as part of broader surgical and restorative preparation.
