What is Esthetic Fillings?

Esthetic fillings are used to restore decayed or worn tooth structure with tooth-colored materials selected to match the surrounding dentition. They are often preferred when maintaining a natural appearance is important alongside function. At Archident, the shape, color, and bite relationship are reviewed carefully before placement.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with cavities or minor tooth fractures
  • People who want tooth-colored restorations
  • Cases needing conservative repair of worn areas
  • Suitable situations after bite and structural assessment

How is the treatment process planned?

After examination and shade selection, the affected area is prepared conservatively and the composite material is layered and shaped to fit the tooth. Bite adjustments and polishing are then completed for comfort and appearance.

What should be considered afterward?

Routine hygiene, avoiding unnecessary force habits, and regular controls help esthetic fillings remain stable and harmonious over time.

Esthetic Fillings FAQ

Frequently asked questions about esthetic fillings

Do esthetic fillings match natural teeth?

Composite materials are selected to blend with the surrounding teeth, and careful shaping helps support a natural look.

Can fillings repair small fractures?

Yes. In suitable cases, esthetic fillings may be used to restore minor chips or structural loss conservatively.

How long do esthetic fillings last?

Longevity depends on oral hygiene, bite forces, and habits such as clenching. Regular controls help monitor their condition.
