What is Orthodontic Treatments?

Orthodontic treatment is planned not only for aesthetics, but also for bite function, easier cleaning, and long-term oral balance. Depending on the case, clear aligners or fixed braces may be considered. At Archident, treatment goals are explained clearly so the patient understands the expected progress and responsibilities.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with crowding or spacing
  • People with bite-related functional concerns
  • Adults seeking discreet alignment options
  • Younger patients needing early orthodontic evaluation

How is the treatment process planned?

Assessment includes photographs, bite analysis, and imaging when necessary. The appropriate system is selected according to alignment needs, oral hygiene habits, and lifestyle expectations, then a follow-up schedule is planned.

What should be considered afterward?

Attending control visits on time, wearing aligners or elastics as instructed, and maintaining careful oral hygiene are essential for predictable progress.

Orthodontic Treatments FAQ

Frequently asked questions about orthodontic treatment

Is orthodontic treatment only for appearance?

No. Orthodontic planning may also improve bite balance, chewing function, speech support, and oral hygiene access.

Can adults have orthodontic treatment?

Yes. Adults may be suitable for orthodontic treatment depending on gum health, bite status, and the type of tooth movement needed.

How do I know whether clear aligners are suitable?

Suitability is determined after examining crowding, bite relationships, treatment goals, and the level of compliance expected from the patient.
