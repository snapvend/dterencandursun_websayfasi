What is Oral Surgery?

Oral surgery covers procedures such as impacted tooth management, advanced implant surgery, and bone-related interventions planned when simpler treatments are not enough. These cases require careful evaluation of anatomy, imaging, symptoms, and healing expectations. At Archident, oral surgery is approached with precision and step-by-step planning.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with impacted or difficult-to-access teeth
  • Cases requiring advanced implant surgery
  • Patients needing pre-prosthetic or bone-related support
  • People with symptoms that require surgical evaluation

How is the treatment process planned?

Planning includes examination, imaging review, and discussion of the expected surgical steps. Procedures are carried out under local anesthesia, and post-operative instructions are adjusted according to the type of surgery performed.

What should be considered afterward?

Following the recommended medication schedule, hygiene guidance, and soft diet instructions helps support recovery and reduce the risk of complications.

Oral Surgery FAQ

Frequently asked questions about oral surgery

When is oral surgery recommended?

It may be recommended for impacted teeth, advanced implant support procedures, or cases where routine restorative treatment alone is not enough.

How is recovery after oral surgery managed?

Recovery depends on the procedure performed. Medication guidance, hygiene recommendations, and follow-up instructions are provided individually.

Is advanced imaging necessary before surgery?

When anatomy or proximity to important structures needs detailed evaluation, advanced imaging may be recommended before surgery.
