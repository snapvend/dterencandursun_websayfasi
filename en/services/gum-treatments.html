What is Gum Treatments?

Gum treatments focus on controlling inflammation, improving tissue health, and reducing the progression of periodontal problems. Bleeding, swelling, recession, bad breath, or sensitivity may indicate that the gums need professional attention. At Archident, gum health is evaluated as a foundation for both preventive and restorative dental care.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with bleeding or swollen gums
  • People experiencing gum recession
  • Cases with chronic plaque-related inflammation
  • Patients preparing for restorative or implant treatments

How is the treatment process planned?

Clinical examination determines the condition of the gums, plaque control, and supporting tissues. Professional cleaning, maintenance planning, and further periodontal treatment steps are then adapted to the findings.

What should be considered afterward?

Daily brushing, interdental cleaning, and follow-up visits are especially important because gum health depends on both clinical treatment and consistent home-care habits.

Gum Treatments FAQ

Frequently asked questions about gum treatment

When should gum problems be evaluated?

If you notice bleeding, swelling, recession, persistent bad breath, or sensitivity around the gums, a professional evaluation is recommended.

Can gum disease affect other dental treatments?

Yes. Gum health is essential for restorative, prosthetic, and implant-related procedures, so periodontal stability is often addressed first.

Does gum treatment only involve cleaning?

Not always. Depending on the condition, treatment may include maintenance planning and additional periodontal steps beyond routine cleaning.
