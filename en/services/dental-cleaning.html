What is Dental Cleaning?

Dental cleaning focuses on removing tartar and plaque accumulation that brushing alone may not eliminate. Regular maintenance helps protect the gums, reduces inflammation risk, and supports fresher breath. At Archident, cleaning is evaluated as part of preventive care, not just a cosmetic step.

Who may be evaluated for this treatment?

  • Patients with visible tartar or plaque buildup
  • People with gum bleeding during brushing
  • Patients seeking preventive maintenance visits
  • Cases needing support before restorative treatment

How is the treatment process planned?

After an examination, plaque and tartar are removed using professional instruments, and the teeth are polished when appropriate. Oral hygiene advice is then adjusted to the patient's needs and home-care habits.

What should be considered afterward?

Regular brushing, flossing, and periodic controls help slow down tartar re-accumulation and support healthier gums between visits.

Dental Cleaning FAQ

Frequently asked questions about dental cleaning

Why is dental cleaning important?

Tartar buildup can contribute to gum inflammation, bad breath, and gum recession. Routine cleaning supports healthier gums and easier home care.

How often should cleaning be done?

The ideal interval depends on tartar buildup, gum condition, and oral hygiene habits. Your dentist can recommend a personalized recall schedule.

Does cleaning harm the enamel?

When performed professionally and only when needed, dental cleaning is intended to remove deposits while supporting oral health.
